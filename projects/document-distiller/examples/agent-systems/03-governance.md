# Governance, Evidence, and Human Trust

Traceability is a product feature for research systems. A reader should be
able to move from a report claim to an evidence chunk and then to the original
document. This chain supports review, debugging, and correction.

The system should distinguish absence of evidence from evidence of absence.
When sources disagree, the report should preserve the disagreement instead of
averaging it into false consensus. When the corpus cannot answer an important
question, the gap belongs in the output.

Human review is most valuable at explicit checkpoints: corpus selection,
pillar inspection, citation review, and acceptance of the final report.
Automation should reduce clerical work without hiding the basis for judgment.
